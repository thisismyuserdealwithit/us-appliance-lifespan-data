# Methodology and limitations

## Purpose

This dataset is designed for consumer planning, explanatory journalism and exploratory analysis. It helps people compare appliance categories, identify common repair questions and see how broad environmental pressures might shift an otherwise national lifespan range. It is not an engineering forecast for a particular serial number and should not be used to approve, deny or price an individual warranty claim.

## National appliance ranges

Each category begins with a national typical value and a deliberately broad low-to-high range. The editorial baseline draws on housing-component life estimates, federal efficiency and safety guidance, appliance service reporting and recent-owner satisfaction research. These sources answer different questions. A first-year service rate is useful evidence about early service demand; an owner-satisfaction study describes a recent ownership experience; neither directly measures how many products remain in service after ten or fifteen years.

Ranges are therefore presented as planning figures rather than false-precision predictions. Simpler layouts, clean ventilation, suitable installation and available repair parts can move an appliance toward the upper end. Heat, mineral scale, moisture, heavy use and an uneconomic major repair can move it toward the lower end.

## State adjustment model

The state estimates use one transparent calculation:

`modeled state years = national category years × state adjustment factor`

Factors range from 0.91 to 1.04. They reflect a restrained editorial judgment about broad heat, humidity, cold, mineral-water pressure, coastal exposure and service distance. The same factor is applied to the category's typical, low and high values, with each result rounded to one decimal place.

This is a scenario model. It is not built from state warranty claims, manufacturer failure records or a controlled household survey. Conditions within a state can vary greatly, and an indoor installation in one county may be gentler than a garage installation across the road. The state notes should be read as maintenance prompts, not deterministic scores.

## Brand and brand-type research

Brand scores and shortlists are editorial assessments based on the cited public evidence, service practicality, category fit and repair context. They are not laboratory durability grades or failure probabilities. A manufacturer's strength in one category does not automatically transfer to another; this is why the release includes separate long-form research for 25 brand-and-appliance combinations.

The research distinguishes among early service demand, recent-owner satisfaction and long-term working life. When public category-specific evidence is thin, the text says so instead of inventing a numeric conclusion. Local technician coverage and parts availability should still be checked for any shortlisted model.

## Common issues and costs

Common issues describe recurring consumer symptoms and the system likely to need attention. Repair-cost ranges are broad US planning figures. They can change with model design, parts, labour, diagnosis, travel and whether several failed components are found together. Obtain a written local diagnosis before making a repair-or-replace decision.

## Source handling and review date

The source register records what each source measures and its main interpretive limit. Sources were reviewed in July 2026. Links may later move, study methods may change and new model generations may perform differently. Corrections and stronger primary evidence are welcome through GitHub issues.
